🎯 Winning Strategy: What Project to Build
Based on this panel, a few patterns are clear. The judges collectively value: real usability over demos, ASEAN/Vietnam market relevance, multi-model AI sophistication, and clear business logic. Here's how to maximise your score:
Best Track: Consumer & Technology by AWS This track is sponsored by AWS (two AWS judges on the panel: Solutions Architect Vietnam + Account Manager), directly connects to Gabriel Chua (OpenAI), and aligns with what Daniel Nguyen, Alex Nguyen, and Travis Fischer all respond to. It's the broadest track with the most judge crossover.
The winning project archetype looks like this: Build a consumer-facing AI product with a clear Vietnam/ASEAN angle that uses multiple AI providers intelligently (not just one API), ships as a working demo with real UX, and has an obvious path to either revenue or scale. Think: a productivity or EdTech tool that works in Vietnamese and English, uses OpenAI for reasoning, routes through OpenRouter for fallbacks/cost, uses JigsawStack for scraping or OCR, and has a clean developer architecture that Nghi Bui would respect.
Specific tactical tips:
Demo on Day 1 of judging, not just slides. Judges like Daniel Nguyen and Travis Fischer have zero patience for concept decks. A working product beats a polished pitch every time.


Build for a Vietnam-specific pain point. Kai Yong Kang, Trung Huynh, and the Vietnamese founder judges will immediately warm to a product that solves a real local problem — healthcare navigation, business operations for SMEs, Vietnamese language interfaces, etc.


Use OpenRouter and mention the model routing logic in your pitch. Louis Vichy will appreciate it, and it signals sophistication.


Include a speech/voice component. Trung Huynh (Blaze AI) focuses on speech for SEA languages. A voice interface or audio processing layer differentiates you and directly speaks to his work.


Make the code clean and readable. Nghi Bui (Google) will likely review what you built technically. A well-structured repo or architecture diagram earns respect even if the product is simple.


Pitch a business model explicitly. Kai Yong runs an accelerator — he's mentally evaluating every team for FastTrack eligibility. If you can say "here's how this becomes a $X/month business in Vietnam," you're already in a different category.


If you do EdTech, lean into Feynman-method learning. Alex Nguyen literally built his million-dollar product around that concept. Referencing it (tactfully) signals you know your market.
